**Scarcity: The Power of Limitation**

Scarcity is a fundamental principle in economics and psychology, where the perceived value of a product or service increases when its availability is limited. This phenomenon is often referred to as the "scarcity effect." For instance, a study by Worchel, Lee, and Adewole (1975) found that people rated cookies as more desirable when they were scarce compared to when they were abundant.

In marketing, scarcity can be used to create a sense of urgency and exclusivity around a product or service. For example, a company like Amazon might use limited-time offers, exclusive deals, or limited-edition products to create a sense of scarcity, driving consumers to make purchasing decisions more quickly.
